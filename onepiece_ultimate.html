What Is
One Piece?

One Piece is not simply gold or treasure. It is proof — undeniable, physical, world-shaking proof that Gol D. Roger lived, sailed, laughed, and conquered everything. When Roger reached the final island Laugh Tale, he found the treasure left by Joy Boy 800 years before — and he laughed, because he finally understood the whole truth.

The One Piece is the accumulated wealth of all the world's kingdoms — every nation's greatest possession, brought to one location by the ancient giant Joy Boy as both an apology and a promise to the people of Fishman Island and to all the oppressed of the world.

But deeper still: One Piece is the story of human freedom versus divine tyranny. The Void Century reveals that the World Government was built on the erasure of a great civilization — one that believed all people should be free. The Poneglyphs were Joy Boy's message, carved in stone so that even gods could not silence them.

The final truth: Roger could not "inherit the will" — he was born too early. He needed someone to come after him. Someone with the power of the Sun God Nika. Someone who would laugh in the face of everything — even the heavens.

'D' の意志 — The Inherited Will

The Will of D.

Across eight centuries, a single initial reappears in those who challenge fate and authority. Those who carry the name "D" are described by the Gorosei as "natural enemies of God."

Roger laughed at his own execution. Whitebeard died standing upright. Ace smiled at the end. Luffy grins in the face of emperors. Those with the D never kneel — even before death, they smile.

The Will of D is not inherited through blood alone — it is a promise passed through time. Joy Boy made a promise he could not keep. Roger found it 800 years too late. Luffy is the one who will finally fulfill it.

作者について — About the Creator

The God of Pirates

27+Years of One Piece
Eiichiro Oda
尾田 栄一郎

Born on January 1, 1975 in Kumamoto, Japan, Eiichiro Oda showed his passion for drawing pirates as early as age 4. Inspired by Akira Toriyama's Dragon Ball and Mitsutoshi Shimabukuro's Toriko, he submitted his first manga at age 17 to Shueisha — and won recognition immediately.

He apprenticed under Nobuhiro Watsuki (Rurouni Kenshin) and worked as an assistant on multiple manga before debuting One Piece in Weekly Shōnen Jump on July 22, 1997. He famously pitched the idea as "a pirate manga where no one dies easily" — pirates who fight not for conquest, but for freedom and dreams.

Oda is known for his extraordinary worldbuilding — foreshadowing events decades in advance, hiding clues in background art, and layering philosophy and history into what appears to be a simple adventure. He works around the clock, often sleeping only 3 hours a night, yet still finds time to answer fan letters personally.

His greatest quote: "Pirates are evil? The Marines are righteous? These are matters of perspective... Fools who don't respect the past are doomed to repeat it."

制作会社 — The Publishers & Studios

Behind the Legend

📖
Shueisha Inc.

Japan's largest manga publisher. Home of Weekly Shōnen Jump — the magazine that launched Dragon Ball, Naruto, and One Piece. Founded in 1926, Shueisha has published One Piece since Volume 1 in 1997. Their Jump Comics label holds the Guinness World Record for best-selling manga series by a single author.

🎬
Toei Animation

The legendary anime studio behind the One Piece anime adaptation, airing since October 20, 1999. Toei Animation has produced over 1,100+ episodes, 15 films, and countless specials. Known also for Dragon Ball Z, Sailor Moon, and Digimon. The longest-running anime adaptation of a single manga series in modern history.

🌍
Weekly Shōnen Jump

The world's most-read manga anthology magazine, published every Monday in Japan. At its 1994 peak, sold 6.5 million copies weekly. One Piece has run continuously here since 1997, surviving every era of manga. The series has been in the top 3 best-selling manga globally for over 20 consecutive years.
